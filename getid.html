Get Your Kenyan ID

Your identity is your power. Here's how to claim it.

New Applicants

Getting your first Kenyan ID? Follow these steps:

1

Check Eligibility

You must be a Kenyan citizen (by birth or registration) and 18 years or older. Make sure you meet these basic requirements before starting the application process.

2

Gather Required Documents

Collect your original birth certificate, copies of your parents' IDs, death certificate (if applicable), and recent passport photos.

Birth Certificate

Original document required

Parents' IDs

Photocopies needed

Passport Photos

Recent colored photos

3

Visit Registration Office

Head to your nearest Assistant County Commissioner's office or any Huduma Centre to begin your application process.

4

Complete Application

Fill out Form 23 accurately, attach all required documents, and have your fingerprints, photograph, and signature captured by the officer.

5

Wait for Processing

Receive your waiting card and wait 4-8 weeks for your ID to be processed. You'll be notified when it's ready for collection.

Lost or Damaged ID

Need to replace your lost or damaged ID? Here's what to do:

1

Report to Police

Visit your nearest police station to file a report about your lost ID. You'll receive a police abstract which is required for the replacement process.

2

Prepare Documents

Gather your original birth certificate, the police abstract, and a photocopy of your lost ID (if you have one available).

3

Visit Registration Office

Go to any Huduma Centre or your Assistant County Commissioner's office with all the required documents.

4

Apply for Replacement

Fill out the replacement form, provide new biometric data (fingerprints and photo), and pay the KSh 100 replacement fee.
